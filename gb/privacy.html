Data Controller

Summary

Settleasy is designed with privacy as a core principle. All settlement and participant data you create remains stored locally on your device. We do not collect, transmit, or store any of your personal data on our servers. We do not have access to your settlement information, participant details, or any images you add to the app.

Information We Collect and Process

Data You Store Locally on Your Device

When you use Settleasy, the following data is created and stored exclusively on your device using Apple's SwiftData framework:

  • Settlement Information: Names, descriptions, dates, and calculations related to your settlements (e.g., household expenses, flatmate shares).
  • Participant Information: Names and details of participants you add to settlements, including percentage shares.
  • Financial Data: Expense and income items, amounts, currencies, exchange rates, and calculated net balances.
  • Settlement Payments: Records of payments made between participants to balance or "settle up" settlements.
  • Images: Photos you choose to associate with participants, whether selected from your photo library or captured with your camera.

Storage Location: All this data is stored locally on your device only. It is never transmitted to our servers or any third party.

Device Permissions

The App requests permissions for the Photo Library, Camera, and Files only when you actively choose to add images to your settlements. These are stored locally and are not accessible by us.

Your Rights (UK & EEA)

If you are in the UK or the European Economic Area (EEA), you have the following rights regarding your data:

  • Right of Access & Rectification: You can see and change all data directly within the App.
  • Right to Erasure: You can delete any data within the App or by uninstalling it.
  • Right to Withdraw Consent: You can manage permissions in your iOS Settings at any time.

Right to Lodge a Complaint: If you are in the UK, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) (www.ico.org.uk) if you believe your data protection rights have been breached.
